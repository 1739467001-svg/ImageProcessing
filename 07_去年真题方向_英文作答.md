## 2. 英式英语·标准答案模板（按考点，考场直接套）

> 都用英式拼写。每段都是可直接写在卷面上的 model answer。

### A. 迭代阈值法 — Iterative (Isodata) Thresholding · **写步骤**
> The iterative thresholding algorithm selects a threshold automatically:
> 1. **Initialise** the threshold $T$ to the mean grey level of the whole image.
> 2. **Segment** the pixels into two groups using $T$: those $\le T$ and those $> T$.
> 3. **Compute** the mean grey level of each group, $\mu_1$ and $\mu_2$.
> 4. **Update** the threshold: $T = (\mu_1 + \mu_2)/2$.
> 5. **Repeat** steps 2–4 until $T$ no longer changes (it has converged).
>
> The final $T$ is then used to binarise the image (output 1 if pixel $> T$, else 0).

### B. 形态学操作 — Morphological Operations
> **Erosion** ($A \ominus B$): the output pixel is set to 1 **only if the structuring element $B$ fits entirely within the foreground** (every pixel it covers is 1); otherwise 0. It **shrinks** foreground regions and removes small protrusions and noise (equivalent to a local **minimum**).
> **Dilation** ($A \oplus B$): the output pixel is set to 1 **if the structuring element touches any foreground pixel**; otherwise 0. It **grows** foreground regions and fills small holes (a local **maximum**).
> **Opening** = erosion then dilation — removes small objects/spurs. **Closing** = dilation then erosion — fills small gaps and holes.
> The result depends on the **structuring element**: a 3×3 square also affects diagonals, whereas a cross (plus) does not.

### C. 噪声与去噪 — Noise & Denoising
> **Types of noise:** *salt-and-pepper* (impulse) noise appears as random black and white pixels; *Gaussian* noise is additive noise drawn from a normal distribution; noise may be *additive* or *multiplicative* (e.g. speckle).
> **Median filter:** replaces each pixel with the **median** of its neighbourhood (the middle value of the sorted window). Being **non-linear**, it removes **salt-and-pepper** noise effectively while **preserving edges**, because outliers are discarded rather than averaged in.
> **Mean (averaging) filter:** linear; suited to **Gaussian** noise but it **blurs** edges. *Rule: median for impulse noise, mean/Gaussian filter for Gaussian noise.*

### D. 卷积、边缘算子、拉普拉斯 — Convolution, Edge Operators & Laplacian
> **Convolution** slides a kernel over the image, multiplying and summing, with the kernel **flipped 180°**; **correlation** is the same **without flipping**. The course defines the gradient as $G_x = f[i,j{+}1] - f[i,j]$ (right − left), i.e. correlation.
> **First-derivative (gradient) operators** detect edges as **peaks**, e.g. the Prewitt/Sobel $G_x$ kernel $[-1\,0\,1;-1\,0\,1;-1\,0\,1]$. The **gradient magnitude** is $|G|=\sqrt{G_x^2+G_y^2}$ and the **direction** is $\alpha=\arctan(G_y/G_x)$.
> **The Laplacian** is a **second-derivative**, isotropic operator $\nabla^2 f = \partial^2 f/\partial x^2 + \partial^2 f/\partial y^2$, approximated by $[0\,1\,0;\,1\,{-}4\,1;\,0\,1\,0]$. Edges are located at its **zero-crossings**. It is sensitive to noise, so it is often combined with Gaussian smoothing — the **Laplacian of Gaussian (LoG)**.

### E. 双线性插值 — Bilinear Interpolation
> Bilinear interpolation estimates the value at a **non-integer location** from the **four nearest pixels**. With fractional offsets $dx$ (column) and $dy$ (row), interpolate first **along the columns** (top and bottom rows), then **between those two results along the rows**:
> $$I = I_{TL}(1{-}dx)(1{-}dy) + I_{TR}\,dx(1{-}dy) + I_{BL}(1{-}dx)dy + I_{BR}\,dx\,dy.$$
> It is **smoother** than **nearest-neighbour** interpolation, which simply rounds to the closest pixel and produces a blocky result.

### F. 深度学习 + RGB — CNN, Layers & RGB Convolution
> A **convolutional neural network (CNN)** extracts features with **convolutional layers**: each layer convolves the multi-channel input with learnable **kernels**, **sums the responses across channels**, adds a **bias**, and applies a non-linear **activation**, e.g. $\text{ReLU}(x)=\max(0,x)$.
> For an **RGB** input the kernel has **depth 3** (one slice per colour channel); the three channel responses are **summed into a single output** value.
> **Pooling** down-samples: **max pooling** takes the maximum of each window, **average pooling** the mean, **global average pooling** reduces each feature map to one mean value. Pooling lowers dimensionality and gives **translation invariance**.
> A typical **framework/architecture**: $[\text{Conv} + \text{ReLU}]\times n \rightarrow \text{Pool} \rightarrow \dots \rightarrow \text{fully-connected} \rightarrow \text{softmax}$.

### G. 图像分割 — Image Segmentation (overview)
> Segmentation **partitions an image into meaningful regions**. The simplest method is **thresholding** — global, or **iterative/isodata** to choose $T$ automatically (see A). Other methods include **P-tile**, the **watershed** algorithm (a *moving threshold* developed from double thresholding that can separate **touching objects**), **region growing**, and **motion / background subtraction**.

### H. RGB → 颜色 — Colour Conversion (RGB to YUV)
> $$Y = 0.299R + 0.587G + 0.114B \quad(\text{luma})$$
> $$C_b = 128 - 0.169R - 0.331G + 0.500B,\qquad C_r = 128 + 0.500R - 0.418G - 0.081B$$
> The **+128 offset applies to the chroma channels only**. Because the human eye is **less sensitive to colour than to brightness**, the chroma channels can be **sub-sampled** (e.g. 4:2:2) to compress the image.

## 5. 英文卷答题策略
- **"Describe / Explain / State the steps"** 类题 → 用上面 §2 的模板，**分点 + 关键术语**，每步一句。
- **计算题** → 写 formula → substitute → result，**步骤分照拿**；单位、英式拼写到位。
- 不确定拼写时，宁可用**英式**（colour/grey/normalise）——这是老师的标准。
- 术语**英文优先**：写 "erosion shrinks the foreground" 比写中文+硬翻更稳。
